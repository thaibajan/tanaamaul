Ideation session

Personalised health based on your biochemistry: the preventative approach

I'm deep into health and wellbeing. Over the last few years I've worked closely with health coaches and practitioners, and I've seen the same pattern over and over: someone gets blood work done, receives a PDF of 100+ biomarkers, and has no idea what to do with any of it.

I've been deep-diving into Claude and realised it 10x's the entire UX and product development lifecycle. So I used real use cases from what I've observed - typical client lifecycles, how check-ins happen, where the gaps are - and mocked up a concept.

The objective was simple: use AI to enhance the customer journey, not create distance between customer and practitioner to boost margins while the experience suffers. Preventative medicine is the future, and there's so much scope to get this right.

Backstory

Why I believe preventative health is the future

It started during the pandemic. People I'd gone to school with - who'd gone into conventional medicine - were starting to specialise in preventative, root-cause approaches. I discovered Dr Mark Hyman, one of the founders of Function Health, and went deep on his podcasts, books, and audiobooks. It became a North Star for how I think about personal wellbeing.

The natural health path can feel a bit fluffy. Conventional medicine has the rigour but is often reactive. The best practitioners combine both: evidence-based reasoning with a holistic, root-cause angle. That's what motivated this project - how do you make that quality of thinking accessible through technology without losing the nuance?

Process

How Claude changed the way I build

Claude helped with everything from ideation to wireframes, but it took a lot of customisation and back-and-forth to get things right. This was pre-Figma MCP, so I exported from Claude as HTML, used a Figma-to-HTML plugin to bring it in, refined the UI, then brought it back into Claude to keep iterating. I didn't want it to look like something Lovable spat out in five seconds.

I also added habit tracking and coach booking - a lower rate than a doctor, but real human accountability on demand. Combined with pattern-level biomarker insights and a sequenced roadmap, it genuinely gives the user power over their health journey. For the UI, I used Tailwind UI as a base with calm colours and a serif/sans-serif pairing for a clean, clinical feel. This was really just about doing something for fun and seeing how far I could push the concept.

01

Start with what matters today

Most health apps dump you into a wall of data. I wanted the home screen to feel like a coach checking in - your adherence this week, what your body's been doing, the one thing to focus on next.

Cronometer was a huge inspiration - I use it daily for macros and love the micronutrient features. But it becomes really powerful when you combine nutrition data with your biochemistry, plus supplement tracking. Your nutrition, supplements, and blood work - all correlated, feeding into a roadmap so you're always working towards a specific goal.

02

Two-minute check-ins your practitioner actually reads

Having worked alongside practitioners, I've seen how check-ins actually happen - WhatsApp messages, voice notes, the odd email. Information gets lost, context gets missed, and the practitioner asks the same questions every call.

I designed a structured weekly check-in - one question per screen, dial ratings, multi-select for symptoms. Two minutes. The practitioner gets clean data they can review before a call instead of scrolling a chat thread. Technology enhancing the relationship, not replacing it.

03

Protocols are useless if you can't stick to them

The supplement stack alone can be overwhelming - zinc bisglycinate before bed, raw ginger before meals, specific proteins at specific times. Most people fall off within a fortnight.

I kept the habit tracker simple. Weekly grid, streak count, category filters. No gamification guilt trips. Just quiet checkmarks that accumulate - the kind of thing that makes you think "yeah, I can do another week."

04

The right thing at the wrong time can do more harm than good

Most health apps give you everything at once and expect you to figure out sequencing. But order matters. If someone has leaky gut and you throw a parasitic cleanse at them, you can trigger endotoxemia - circulating more toxins when the body hasn't recovered. The right intervention in the wrong phase sets people back.

That's why the roadmap is phasic. One phase at a time, in the right order. Rebuild foundations first - digestion, nutrient absorption, daily rhythm - before optimisation. The "20% that gets 80% of results" section surfaces the highest-leverage habits so people know exactly where to start.

05

Patterns, not isolated numbers

I've had extensive blood work done myself. What actually works is looking at biomarkers through the lens of patterns and correlations - not one at a time - with the aim of longevity, not just disease avoidance. What's classed as "normal" testosterone today is historically very low compared to our ancestors.

I grouped everything into body systems - heart, metabolic, hormonal, liver, immune - with a traffic-light status on each. The real value is in the connections: elevated fasting glucose alongside raised HbA1c and HOMA-IR tells a different story than any one alone. Plain-language explanations backed by the latest research, optimised for longevity rather than just "within range."
